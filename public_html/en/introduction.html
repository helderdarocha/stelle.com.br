Introdução

This site contains an adaptation of the epic poem called Hell (Inferno) by the Florentine poet Dante Alighieri (1265-1321). Hell is the first of the three long poems that complete the Comedy (later Divine Comedy) – Dante’s masterpiece, that was written between 1307 and 1321, in Italian. The Comedy is a Christian allegory which describes, in rhymed verses, a journey through Hell, Purgatory and Paradise. It is divided in 100 chapters which are called Cantos. The first 34 Cantos narrate the voyage through Hell.
